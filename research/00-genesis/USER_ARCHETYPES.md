## Primary Archetype: "The Anxious Preparer"

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28-42
- **Location:** Major US metros (NYC, LA, Miami, Houston, Chicago)
- **Origin countries:** India, China, Mexico, Philippines, Brazil, Nigeria
- **Education:** College-educated (often advanced degrees)
- **Income:** $50K-$150K household
- **Tech comfort:** High (smartphone native, uses apps daily)

### Situation
- Married to US citizen or green card holder
- Interview scheduled 2-8 weeks out
- Has done extensive Googling already
- Active on Reddit r/immigration and immigration forums
- Already hired (or is considering) an immigration lawyer

### Psychology
```
Emotions: Anxiety, fear, hope, frustration
Self-talk: "What if I say the wrong thing?"
          "What if they think we're not really in love?"
          "I've waited 18 months for this interview"
          "My whole future depends on this"
```

### Behaviors
- Searches "green card interview questions" 3-5x per week
- Reads every Reddit post about interview experiences
- Practices with spouse but arguments ensue
- Wakes up at 3am thinking about questions
- Has a folder with all documents organized

### Needs
1. **Confidence**: Feel ready, not just prepared
2. **Realism**: Practice that mimics actual stress
3. **Privacy**: Don't want to share fears with others
4. **Validation**: Know they're on the right track
5. **Efficiency**: Limited time, maximum impact

### Willingness to Pay
- Has already spent $2K-$5K on lawyer
- Would pay $50-$200 for quality prep
- Free options tried, want "the best"
- Price signals quality for high-stakes decisions

### Objections
- "Is this legal? Can I practice interview answers?"
- "Will this actually help or make me more nervous?"
- "Can an AI understand my specific situation?"
- "What if I become too rehearsed?"

### Where They Are
- Reddit: r/immigration, r/USCIS, r/greencard
- YouTube: Searching interview experience vlogs
- Google: "green card interview tips 2024"
- Immigration lawyer websites
- Visa Journey forums

## Secondary Archetype: "The Supportive Spouse"

### Situation
- US citizen or green card holder spouse
- Wants to help partner prepare
- Feels helpless watching spouse's anxiety
- May be less emotionally invested (already has status)

### Needs
1. **Tools**: Something to DO to help
2. **Understanding**: What will spouse be asked?
3. **Alignment**: Make sure stories match
4. **Support**: Be a good practice partner

### Behaviors
- Takes charge of scheduling practice sessions
- Often the one who finds and purchases prep tools
- Wants to feel like they're contributing

---

## Tertiary Archetype: "The DIY Researcher"

### Situation
- Can't afford lawyer
- Doing entire process themselves
- USCIS website + Reddit is their guide
- Higher stakes because no professional backup

### Needs
1. **Affordability**: Every dollar counts
2. **Comprehensiveness**: All-in-one resource
3. **Trust**: Is this source reliable?
4. **Community**: Others in same situation

### Behaviors
- Spends hours on free resources
- Hesitant to pay but will for clear value
- Shares resources with others in same boat
- Active forum participant (gives back)

---

## Anti-Personas (Who We're NOT For)

### "The Fraud Case"
- Seeking to game the system
- Wants coaching on "right" answers
- Looking for ways to deceive adjudicators
- **We actively don't want this user**

### "The Over-Preparer"
- Already done 100+ practice sessions
- Diminishing returns from more practice
- May need anxiety treatment, not prep tools
- **We guide them to recognize when enough is enough**

### "The Corporate User"
- Immigration law firms seeking bulk tools
- HR departments doing employee immigration
- **Phase 6 opportunity, not MVP focus**

## Jobs To Be Done

### Functional Jobs
1. Practice answering interview questions out loud
2. Identify inconsistencies between spouse answers
3. Learn what questions to expect
4. Get feedback on answer quality
5. Track preparation progress

### Emotional Jobs
1. Reduce anxiety about the unknown
2. Feel confident walking into USCIS
3. Believe I've done everything I can
4. Transform fear into preparedness
5. Feel supported, not alone

### Social Jobs
1. Show spouse I'm taking this seriously
2. Be able to tell family "I'm prepared"
3. Not be embarrassed by lack of preparation
4. Join community of others in same situation

---

## Key Insight

> **Users don't want more information. They want transformation.**
>
> From: Anxious, uncertain, unprepared
> To: Confident, ready, practiced
>
> **We sell the transformation, not the tool.**
